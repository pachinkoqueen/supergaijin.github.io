Build better products through qualitative user research

No Harvard Business School case study ended with “ … and the company failed because they simply knew too much about their users.”

This site is a hands-on guide to conducting high-quality, remote, facilitated user research. Ultimately, you will have meaningful 30-45 minute conversations with around 8-10 carefully-selected users in the span of one week.

High-quality user research is

Thoughtful

Research conducted with precision and rigor results in solid recommendations the entire team can stand behind.

Habitual

The goal is not to perform research once, but make it a regular habit whether it's once a sprint, once a week, or twice a month.

Speedy

Late research is like yesterday's news. Luckily, speed comes with time and practice.

What you'll need

The materials created throughout this process will become the foundation for future rounds of testing, so you won't have to start from scratch every time.

Your team

Start with a small core team; as research becomes a regular habit, more team members will join. An open, optimistic attitude towards learning is invaluable.

Software

  • Conferencing app (ex GoTo Meeting)
    Videoconferencing is not necessary. However, you will need a suite allowing users to easily join your meeting, and lets users control your screen.
  • Email marketing app (ex MailChimp)
    Recruiting emails are easier to send and track with an email marketing app as opposed to an individual's email address.
  • Screening/recruiting tool (ethn.io)
    ethn.io is a unique product that combines screening, scheduling, and incentive fulfillment. Worth paying for a small plan, just to save the headache of sorting through user responses and manual scheduling. I don't work for ethn.io, I just love their product.

Hardware

  • Extra monitor (recommended)
  • External hard drive (several gig capacity)

User Incentives

Amazon email gift cards are the easiest incentive. For 30 minutes of time, a $20 gift card is enough of an incentive. For 8-10 users, budget $160-$200 per round.
