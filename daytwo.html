At the end of this course, you will have conducted meaningful 30-45 minute conversations with around 8-10 carefully selected users.

Schedule Recruits

As the ethn.io schedule fills up in ethn.io, start picking and scheduling them. Exciting! Expect one or two no-shows or cancellations; I've found it helpful to schedule more interviews the first day than the subsequent day(s).

Testing will likely take place over several days. You now have permission to hate me for making this seem like a single day.

TIP For 30 minute interviews, schedule an hour. This gives some breathing room to accommodate late-shows, technology burps, or gregarious users.

Use ethn.io to send out confirmations, or send them on your own.

Consent Form

Because you are recording your interviews, you will need your users' consent for each session. If they don't sign beforehand, have them do it before you start your session before you start recording.

TIP Use Google Docs to host a basic boilerplate consent form

Set Up Room

Don't waste valuable time fiddling with a monitor or troubleshooting your conferencing tool. QA the setup before your session by having a colleague dial in. Test what they see, what you will see, and what happens when you hand over control of your screen.

Set up (adjust as needed)

Laptop with external hard drive Screen recordings can become enormous in size; save them to the hard drive

Quicktime (Mac) After the user signs the consent form, go to Quicktime File > New Screen Recording

Monitor A (“Onstage”) This is what the user will see during the session, and what Quicktime will be recording

Monitor B (“Offstage”) Any items (another prototype for example) that you do not want the user to see

Sticky notes and/or Whiteboard Have plenty of sticky notes and pens for observers

Clear your browser cache or create a browser profile for testing purposes

Refine Script

Go through the flow of your script. Personally, I like to write out the script, not so much to read line-by-line but as a reminder not to forget anything. Remember, once you come up with a script you can easily reuse it for future sessions.

Add a few new sections to the script:

Introduction

Start off each session thanking the user for their time, and ask a few questions, such as where the user works, what they do, perhaps how long they've used your product. The purpose is to warm up both you and your user. Next, give a bit of background as to what you will be doing today. I like to reiterate that I want them to be honest with me, and assure them I didn't design this product so nothing they say will hurt my feelings. Also mention you'll ask them to think out loud whenever possible. Before you show the user your prototype you want them to feel comfortable with giving feedback.

There is no need to introduce anyone else observing. You want your user to feel at ease, not like they're on a stage.

Test Cues

Add any prompts, such as START RECORDING, so you don't forget. Review your questions; keep them open-ended, to get to the “why.” The goal is to observe users interacting with your product, not to instruct them how to use it.

Example

Instead of

  • This product is pretty cool, isn't it?
  • How often will you use it?
  • Don't you like it?
  • Let me show you how this works.

Ask

  • What do you think this means?
  • Tell me more about what you mean by that.
  • What do you think will happen next?
  • Is that what you expected to have happen? Why / why not?
  • How are you feeling at this point?

Wrap Up

As you wrap up after your discovery questions, graciously thank users for their time. Let them know when to expect their incentive and what to do if they do not.

QA Prototype

User tests can veer off course due to broken links or misspellings. Just as you would QA your test setup, QA your prototype as well.

Use real data instead of pop culture references and silly names. For example, if a screen contains a list of other users, don't use Game of Thrones cast members or Ed Sheeran and Lady Gaga as placeholders.

QA Prototype: Complete

Remind Team

Send a quick update your team with the date(s) and times, and reiterate testing norms:

  • Show up on time as to not disrupt testing in progress
  • Stay in "silent observation" mode; under no circumstance should anyone interrupt the test
  • If anyone has a question, write it on a sticky note and pass it to you

Keep it upbeat and light - get the team excited about learning and having their burning questions answered!
